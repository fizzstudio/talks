LLM Summaries: prose and cons

This line chart compares passenger car sales in four countries—China, the United States, Japan, and Germany—from 2005 to 2018.

  • China’s line starts low, around 4 million cars in 2005, then soars dramatically, peaking near 25 million by 2017, before dipping slightly in 2018.
  • The U.S. stays mostly steady, fluctuating between 6 and 8 million, dipping during the 2008 recession, then recovering slightly before declining again after 2016.
  • Japan remains nearly flat, around 4 to 5 million each year.
  • Germany holds steady near 3 million throughout the period.

Overall, the key story is China’s explosive growth, while the other countries’ sales remain relatively stable or slowly decline.
